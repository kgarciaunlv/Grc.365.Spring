Hawaii Island Animals

This list highlights 20 unique animals found on Hawaii Island, along with where they are most commonly seen and a fun fact about each one.

# Animal Location on Island Fun Fact
1 Hawaiian Green Sea Turtle (Honu) Punaluʻu Black Sand Beach They can hold their breath for up to 5 hours while resting underwater.
2 Nēnē (Hawaiian Goose) Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park It is the official state bird of Hawaii.
3 Hawaiian Monk Seal Kona Coast They are one of the most endangered seal species in the world.
4 Manta Ray Keauhou Bay They have the largest brain-to-body ratio of any fish.
5 Spinner Dolphin Kona Coast They spin in the air to communicate and remove parasites

6 Humpback Whale Kohala Coast (Winter Months) They migrate over 3,000 miles to Hawaii each year to breed.
7 Hawaiian Hoary Bat (‘Ōpe‘ape‘a) Forests near Hilo It is Hawaii’s only native land mammal.
8 Jackson’s Chameleon Rainforests near Hilo Males have three horns and can move their eyes independently.
9 Wild Goat Mauna Kea Slopes Goats were introduced in the 1700s by explorers.
10 Wild Boar Hamakua Forests They were brought by Polynesians over 1,000 years ago.
11 Hawaiian Hawk (‘Io) Island-wide Forest Areas This hawk exists only on the Big Island.
12 Gold Dust Day Gecko Gardens and Coastal Areas Its bright colors help it blend into tropical plants.
13 Blacktip Reef Shark Shallow Coastal Waters They are harmless to humans and prefer small fish.
14 Yellow Tang Kona Reefs They are one of the most popular aquarium fish in the world.
15 Hawaiian Happy Face Spider Rainforests Some have markings that look like a smiling face.
16 Moray Eel Lava Rock Reefs They open and close their mouths constantly to breathe.
17 Cardinal Honeycreeper (‘I‘iwi) High Elevation Forests Its curved beak is perfect for sipping nectar from native flowers.
18 Hawaiian Damselfly Streams and Wetlands One of the few native insects still surviving today.
19 Sea Cucumber Tide Pools They help keep reefs clean by eating sand and organic matter.
20 Octopus (Heʻe) Rocky Shorelines They can change color instantly to camouflage themselves.
